The Street Food Trail

Five plates that define this city

Eaten in this order on one evening — the most authentic ₹300 you'll ever spend in Odisha. Stalls fire up from around 5 PM.

01
ଦହିବରା ଆଳୁଦମ

Dahibara Aludum

Lentil baras soaked in tempered curd water, drowned in spicy aludum and ghuguni. Invented in Cuttack, perfected everywhere. The undisputed king.

📍 Rajmahal Square · Saheed Nagar stalls
02
ଗୁପଚୁପ

Gupchup

Odisha's answer to panipuri — crisper shells, spicier water, and a free "dry one" at the end if the bhaiya likes you. Non-negotiable evening ritual.

📍 Every market square · Unit 4 is legendary
03
ବରା ଘୁଗୁନି

Bara Ghuguni

Crispy fried lentil baras smothered in tangy yellow pea curry, topped with onions and sev. The tiffin staple locals never outgrow.

📍 Bapuji Nagar · Master Canteen area
04
ଛେନା ପୋଡ଼

Chhena Poda

"Burnt cheese" — caramelised baked cottage cheese with cardamom. Odisha's signature dessert, said to be Lord Jagannath's favourite.

📍 Any good sweet shop · District Center
05
ପଖାଳ

Pakhala Bhata

Fermented rice in water with fried sides — the dish of Odia summers, now served as full ceremonial thalis at lunch.

📍 Atithi Devo Bhaba, Saheed Nagar (lunch)

Visitor Essentials

Land here, know everything

The practical stuff nobody puts in one place — until now.

✈️

Getting in

Biju Patnaik International Airport (BBI) is just 5 km from the city centre. Trains arrive at Bhubaneswar station, right beside Master Canteen square.

Airport → city: ~₹250 cab
🚌

Getting around

Mo Bus is the award-winning city bus network (use the Mo Bus app). Autos run everywhere; Ola, Uber and Rapido all operate, with Rapido bikes cheapest for solo hops.

Mo Bus from ₹10
🌤️

Best time to visit

October to February is ideal — pleasant days, festival season, and migratory birds at Chilika. Summers (Apr-Jun) are hot; carry water and plan indoor afternoons.

Peak season: Oct - Feb
🗣️

Speak a little Odia

Namaskar = hello · Dhanyabad = thank you · Kete? = how much? · Bhala = good. Hindi and English work fine across the city.

ନମସ୍କାର · Namaskar
🛕

Temple etiquette

Dress modestly at temples. Lingaraj and Puri Jagannath allow Hindus only inside; phones and leather stay outside (safe stands provided). Mornings are calmest.

Remove footwear before entering
🆘

Emergency & help

All-in-one emergency: 112 · Ambulance: 108 · Police: 100. Major hospitals: AIIMS Bhubaneswar, KIMS, Apollo. Tap water — stick to bottled.

Dial 112
